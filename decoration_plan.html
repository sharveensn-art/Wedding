Step 02

Temple Layout — Bird's Eye View

Based on your temple images, here are the key structural zones and their existing features:

Om Backdrop (Focal Point)

Dark reddish-brown panel with the Om symbol — the spiritual centrepiece. Needs only minimal framing, not covering.

Brass Bells & Lattice Ceiling

Geometric carved ceiling with hanging brass bells. Existing decor — complement, don't compete.

Mandap / Homa Platform

The raised ritual platform with brass diya ring. Keep this zone sacred and uncluttered.

Stone Columns (8 total)

Ornate carved stone pillars — perfect for vertical floral wrapping or garland draping.

Central Aisle

White walkway leading from entrance to mandap — the procession path. Key visual corridor.

Entrance / Welcome Zone

First impression area. Flanked by columns. Sets the colour story before guests enter.

Step 03

Zone-by-Zone Decoration Plan

Inspired by your deco samples, adapted to the temple's sacred character. Each zone is designed to feel intentional — never busy.

Zone 01

Welcome Area

  • Wooden easel with a calligraphy welcome sign on ivory card stock — names in gold script
  • 2 brass urns (low, wide) on a marble slab, filled with deep red roses & trailing greenery
  • 4 brass conical pillars (as in your sample) flanking the sign
  • Rose petals in a loose crescent on the floor
Deep Red Roses Eucalyptus Brass Vessels
Zone 02

Aisle Walkway

  • White jasmine garlands hanging vertically from the lattice ceiling — 3 strands, each tipped with a red rose cluster
  • Thin ivory organza runners laid along the aisle edges
  • Brass diyas placed every 1.5m along the aisle floor (both sides)
  • No standing flower arrangements — keep it open and processional
White Jasmine Red Rose Tips Brass Diyas
Zone 03

Stone Columns

  • Lower half of each column wrapped in a single jasmine garland strand — simple spiral wrap
  • At capital (top) of each column: one tight red rose cluster, no ribbon
  • No full draping — let the carved stone texture breathe
  • Symmetry is key: all 8 columns treated identically
White Jasmine Red Rose Cluster
Zone 04

Mandap & Om Backdrop

  • Do NOT decorate the Om panel itself — let it remain the focal point
  • Frame the backdrop with a simple arch of white button chrysanthemums and sage leaves — thin, airy, not dense
  • Two tall brass floor lamps (standing) on either side of the backdrop
  • Existing diya ring on the platform — supplement with marigold petals scattered around the base
  • Optional: a low, thin jasmine garland as a border around the platform edge
Om — untouched White Chrysanthemum Sage Leaves Brass Floor Lamps
Zone 05

Seating Flanks

  • Ivory chair sashes (simple knot, no bow) on aisle-side chairs
  • End-of-row markers: one small brass vase with 3 red roses + eucalyptus per aisle seat
  • No floral table centrepieces — the mandap is the centrepiece
Ivory Sashes Red Roses Mini Brass Vases
Zone 06

Ambient Lighting

  • Warm white fairy lights woven through the ceiling lattice — not coloured
  • The brass bells should remain visible; no lighting behind them
  • Candles in glass hurricanes along the window sills
  • No uplighting or coloured gels — the crimson tones come from florals, not light

Step 04

Design Principles

◯

Sacred First

The temple's existing elements — Om, brass bells, diya ring — are the decor. Everything added must defer to them.

—

Restraint

One flower type per zone. Maximum three colours in any single view. Negative space is intentional.

◆

Saree Echo

Deep red florals mirror the bride's crimson. Silver-white jasmine mirrors the zari embroidery. The bride is the palette.

↑

Vertical, Not Horizontal

Hang garlands from above. Avoid low table arrangements that block sightlines to the mandap.

✦

Natural Materials Only

Real flowers, real brass, real stone. No artificial flowers, no plastic, no synthetic ribbons.

○

Scent Awareness

Jasmine is fragrant. Use it in the aisle and columns. Keep the mandap zone scent-neutral so incense remains distinct.

Step 05

Do's & Don'ts

Do

  • Use deep red roses and white jasmine as the hero florals
  • Let the Om backdrop be visible from every seat
  • Work with the existing brass and stone tones
  • Keep the aisle completely clear of obstacles
  • Use warm (not cool) lighting temperature throughout
  • Add fresh marigold petals on the ritual platform floor
  • Keep the ivory base colour consistent throughout all zones

Don't

  • Add pink or orange tones — they clash with the bride's crimson
  • Drape fabric over the Om panel or obscure it
  • Use artificial flowers anywhere visible
  • Hang dense floral walls — they compete with the carved stone
  • Use coloured light gels (blue, purple) — they alter the crimson florals
  • Over-decorate the mandap platform — it must stay ritually functional
  • Mix gold and silver metal finishes — stick to brass/champagne gold only
